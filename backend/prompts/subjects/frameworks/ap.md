EXAM FRAMEWORK: Advanced Placement (AP) by College Board

Key characteristics:
- Curriculum is defined by the College Board Course and Exam Description (CED)
- Exams typically have two sections: Multiple Choice and Free Response
- Multiple Choice: ~40-60 questions, ~55-105 minutes, machine-scored
- Free Response: ~2-7 questions, ~45-120 minutes, human-scored with rubrics
- Scoring: 1-5 scale (5=Extremely well qualified, 3=Qualified)
- Content must align with AP curriculum framework units and learning objectives
- Questions test conceptual understanding, reasoning, and application

When generating content for AP:
- Structure units to match AP CED unit numbering
- Include AP-style MCQ with 4 answer choices (A, B, C, D)
- Free response questions should have multi-part structure (a, b, c, d)
- Reference AP scoring guidelines when creating rubrics

Subject-specific AP exam formats:
- STEM (Calculus, Physics, Chemistry, Biology): MC + FRQ with calculator/non-calculator sections
- History (APUSH, World, Euro): MC + Short Answer Questions (SAQ) + Document-Based Question (DBQ) + Long Essay Question (LEQ)
- English (Lang, Lit): MC on passages + 3 essay prompts (synthesis/rhetorical analysis/argument OR poetry/prose/literary argument)
- Social Sciences (Psych, Econ, Gov): MC + FRQ; Psychology has 100 MC questions
- World Languages: MC (reading + listening) + FRQ (writing + speaking including interpersonal and presentational tasks)
- Environmental Science: MC + 3 FRQ with data analysis and experimental design

When generating study guides and quizzes, match the EXACT format of the specific AP exam being studied.
