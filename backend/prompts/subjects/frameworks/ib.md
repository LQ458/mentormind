EXAM FRAMEWORK: International Baccalaureate (IB) Diploma Programme

Key characteristics:
- Two-year programme with Internal Assessment (IA) and External Assessment
- Courses offered at Standard Level (SL) and Higher Level (HL)
- HL covers additional topics and requires deeper analysis
- Scoring: 1-7 scale per subject (7=Excellent), total 45 points with TOK/EE bonus
- Assessment includes Paper 1, Paper 2, and sometimes Paper 3 (HL only)
- Internal Assessment varies by subject (lab reports, mathematical explorations, historical investigations, etc.)
- Emphasis on international-mindedness, critical thinking, and interdisciplinary connections

When generating content for IB:
- Clearly distinguish SL and HL content — mark HL-only topics
- Include command terms from IB taxonomy (define, describe, explain, analyse, evaluate, discuss)
- Structure questions to match IB paper formats (short answer, structured, extended response)
- Reference IB assessment criteria and markschemes
- Include Theory of Knowledge (TOK) connections where relevant
- For sciences: include Data-Based Questions (DBQ) that require graph interpretation
- For humanities: include source-based questions and essay prompts with markband descriptors
