EXAM FRAMEWORK: A Level (Cambridge International / CIE)

Key characteristics:
- Syllabus defined by Cambridge Assessment International Education
- Typically split into AS Level (Year 1) and A2 Level (Year 2)
- Papers: Usually 2-4 papers depending on subject
- Structured questions with marks allocated per step
- Mark schemes are very specific: each correct step earns marks
- Questions progress from straightforward to challenging within each paper
- Calculator policy varies by paper

When generating content for A Level:
- Structure units to match Cambridge syllabus chapter numbering
- Include structured questions with mark allocations [2], [3], [4]
- Show mark scheme notation: M1 (method), A1 (accuracy), B1 (independent)
- Questions should progress in difficulty within each set
